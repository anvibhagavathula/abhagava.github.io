About Me

Hello! I'm Anvita Bhagavathula, a senior at Brown University pursuing a degree in Physics (Sc.B) and Applied Mathematics (A.B).

I am interested in researching simulation techniques, such as molecular dynamics and density functional theory, to study physical systems. I want to explore how we can combinine simulations with AI/ML techniques to better predict the behavior of these systems and accelerate the drug or materials discovery processes. I am also interested in exploring how we can make these AI/ML architecutres more domain-informed, interpretable, and trustworthy.

I am currently applying for PhD programs in Applied Mathematics and Computational Science & Engineering. I am also applying to 1-year residency programs and internships to help me hone my research abilities before I start my PhD.

I am a part of Brown's low-dimensional electronics lab where I am studying the properties of twisted multilayer graphene systems using density functional theory for my senior thesis. I am also a research assistant at The Crunch Group where I am designing and implementing self-adaptive PINNs (physics-informed neural networks) to solve differential equations in data-poor regimes. In the past, I have worked with the Research for Industry group at Microsoft Research where I developed an interpretable machine-learning based model to predict food protein digestibility using a QSAR (quantitative structure-activity relationship) approach. More details about my research projects can be found here.

In my free time, I love to play the bass, sing, paint and do film photography. I also love cooking and creating recipes for my friends and family.

Email: anvita_bhagavathula@brown.edu
