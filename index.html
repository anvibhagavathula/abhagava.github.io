About Me

Hello! I'm Anvita Bhagavathula, a senior at Brown University pursuing a degree in Physics (Sc.B) and Applied Mathematics (A.B). I have had a range of research experiences across industry and academia where I have worked to develop computational solutions to solve problems in fields such as laser communications technology and condensed matter physics. More details on these experiences can be found in my CV.

My experiences sparked my passion for making connections across disciplines to solve problems and drive tangible change through my work. Specifically, I am interested in researching how we can thoughtfully use computation to address problems in science and engineering: How can we use atomistic simulation techniques, such as molecular dynamics and density functional theory, to study microscale and macroscale behavior of physical systems? How can we combine these simulations with AI/ML techniques to better predict the behavior of these systems and accelerate the drug or materials discovery process? And how can we ensure that these AI/ML architectures are domain-informed, interpretable, and trustworthy?

I am applying for PhD programs in Applied Mathematics and Computational Science & Engineering. I am also applying to 1-year residency programs or internships to help me hone my research abilities before I start my PhD.

I am currently a part of Brown's low-dimensional electronics lab where I am studying the properties of twisted multilayer graphene systems using density functional theory for my senior thesis. I am also a research assistant at The Crunch Group where I am designing and implementing self-adaptive PINNs (physics-informed neural networks) to solve differential equations in data-poor regimes. In the past, I have worked with the Research for Industry group at Microsoft Research where I developed an interpretable machine-learning based model to predict food protein digestibility using a QSAR (quantitative structure-activity relationship) approach. More details about my research projects can be found here.

In my free time, I love to play the bass, sing, paint and do film photography. I also love cooking and developing recipes for my friends and family.
